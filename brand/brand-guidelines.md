# Pilin' It Higher Farms — Brand Guidelines

## Who We Are

Pilin' It Higher Farms is a small-batch cannabis cultivation operation in Duxbury, Vermont. Founded by Greg and Danielle Domina and co-owned by Nick Donnelly, the farm takes its name from a Disco Biscuits song — which tells you everything about who we are and where we come from.

Greg has been growing cannabis in Vermont for as long as he can remember. Exactly how long that is, we'll leave between Greg and the calendar. Now he's doing it with a license, carefully, and with the same hands-on craft he's always brought to his garden. Nick brings his own deep roots to the operation — an almost unfair amount of experience in what Vermont insists on calling a new industry. He's also fronted more than a few successful jam bands across this state over the years, which given the company he keeps, should surprise exactly no one. Vermont Tier 2 license, four greenhouses, up to 312 plants. Small enough to know every plant. Big enough to matter.

---

## The Name

"Pilin' It Higher" is a Disco Biscuits song. Greg is the bassist for Vermont jam band The Edd. Music isn't a marketing angle for this brand — it's the culture that shaped the people who built it. References to jam bands, concerts, and Vermont's music community belong in this brand the same way soil and seasons do.

---

## Voice & Tone

**The short version:** A Vermont farmer who's been to a lot of shows. Knows their craft. Doesn't need to explain it. Laughs easily.

**Voice pillars:**
- **Authentic** — No hype, no gloss. If Greg wouldn't say it, don't write it.
- **Music-rooted** — References to jam culture feel natural, never forced.
- **Vermont-proud** — Seasons, soil, small-town craft. This is a Vermont farm, not a cannabis brand.
- **Understated confidence** — The work speaks. We don't shout.
- **Warmly irreverent** — Humor is welcome. Nothing crass.

## What We Grow

**Snowberry** — Our flagship. Decade-old mother plant. Bred for Vermont — mold-resistant, climate-adapted. An heirloom in every sense.

**Zerbtane** — Our highest-THC offering. Oregon genetics, Vermont soil.

Plus three additional strains rotated seasonally.

## What We Don't Do

- We don't use cannabis slang as a substitute for actual description
- We don't make health claims
- We don't sound corporate
- We don't market to anyone under 21
- We don't use stock photography of strangers being stoned

---

## Compliance

All marketing complies with Vermont Cannabis Control Board advertising rules. Age gate required on all digital entry points. No health or medical claims. No specific effect claims. Required disclosures included on all materials.

## The Pitch (one paragraph version)

Pilin' It Higher Farms is a small-batch cannabis operation in Duxbury, Vermont, founded by Greg and Danielle Domina and co-owned by Nick Donnelly. Greg spent decades as a legacy cultivator before navigating the Vermont licensing process to go legal in 2024. Named after a Disco Biscuits song, the farm runs four greenhouses and two signature strains — including Snowberry, an heirloom variety with a decade-old mother plant adapted to Vermont's climate. Greg is also the bassist for Vermont jam band The Edd, and Nick can hold his own with a guitar. The farm is currently available through Mountain Girl Cannabis in Rutland.
